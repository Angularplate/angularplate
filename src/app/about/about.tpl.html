What is Angularplate?

Angularplate is an AngularJS project using best-practices, which also makes use of modern, cutting edge technologies.

The goal of this project is to provide people either interested in getting into AngularJS or those using AngularJS to have a point of reference that they can use when working on their own projects.

The idea is to provide the AngularJS community with more information and transparency as to what other developers in the community are using on their projects and to potentially give advice or information to those unsure of the best/potential solution(s) to the problems they may face during development.

Why on Earth did you make this monstrosity?

To put it simply. I wanted to.

I had looked at other AngularJS guidelines and projects and felt that something had been missed/overlooked in regard to methodology, structure, technologies used etc. within each of them.

As such I felt that with help from the other great projects, guides and resources available for AngularJS, that I'd be able to attempt my own best practice project, that both I and others would be able to make use of.

Who... are... you?

Hey, Matt here (Matthew if you prefer) and I'm currently the main contributor and the author of the Angularplate project.

Angularplate is intended to be a foundation from which people can create their own projects and in-turn contribute back to Angularplate itself by finding best-practice solutions to problems and to find technologies/methodologies that could be employed within both the AngularJS and general web communities.

I hope for this project to catch the interest of like-minded developers that can contribute great ideas and/or resources to the project and help both the AngularJS and web communities in doing so.

Enough already, what does this use?

In short, the best methodologies, practices and most appropriate technologies.

Angularplate takes best practice methodologies from various sources (structure, JavaScript syntax/usage, HTML syntax/usage, etc.) and bundles it with some of the latest technologies used today such as

  • Gulp/GulpJS
  • AngularJS
  • NodeJS
  • Karma
  • Bower
  • Sass
  • Travis

All the technologies used here have a great deal to offer to the project and weren't just included on a whim or them being popular at the time of creation.

Please feel free to put forward or offer any additional technologies that you use or feel would be useful for use within this project and specify why (for bonus points and clarity).
